About Me

I am a motivated, detail-oriented learner who has built a strong technical foundation through my Bachelor of Science in Software Engineering at Brigham Young University–Idaho, where I graduated with a 3.963 GPA and led an embedded IoT thermostat senior project using Arduino, C++, sensors, and cloud integration. My background combines technical problem-solving with years of professional experience in administrative and accounting roles at organizations such as the University of Texas at Austin and Burks Reprographics, which strengthened my skills in organization, documentation, data accuracy, and collaboration. After dedicating many years as a stay-at-home mother since 2004, I returned to advanced study and am currently pursuing a Master’s degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering with a focus on Software Engineering at the University of Texas at Austin. Alongside my studies, I serve as a Service Missionary for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, where I facilitate BYU-Idaho Pathway classes, reflecting my commitment to mentorship, leadership, and lifelong learning.

I’m currently a graduate student working through web fundamentals so I can build React apps with a strong foundation.
